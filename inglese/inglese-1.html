[Home](../index.html) ## Romantic Age - Industrial revolution and French revolution as important events - Shift from reason to feelings as the main value - Nature becomes a source of inspiration and emotions - Rejection of strict rules in poetry and embrace of individualism - Social inequality and exploitation due to industrialization ## Augustan Age - Age of reason and adherence to strict rules - Focus on recreating classical style and harmony - Poetic diction and adherence to rules in language ## William Blake - Poet and engraver committed to highlighting the consequences of the industrial revolution - Two collections of poems: Songs of Innocence and Songs of Experience - Innocence represents the way children approach life, while experience represents the way adults approach life - Symbolism and metaphors used in his poems ## Wordsworth - Supporter of French revolution and advocate for the return to nature - Nature as a source of inspiration and happiness - Simple language and focus on ordinary situations - Poet as a sensitive observer of hidden beauty ## Coleridge - Cooperator with Wordsworth in writing Lyrical Ballads - Use of opium influenced his imaginative features in poetry - Rhyme of the Ancient Mariner as a supernatural ballad - Suspension of disbelief and focus on extraordinary events
