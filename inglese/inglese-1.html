[Home](../index.html) # Romantic Age - Industrial, French and American revolution as important events - Shift from reason to feelings as the main value - Nature becomes a source of inspiration and emotions - Rejection of strict rules in poetry and embrace of individualism - Social inequality and exploitation due to industrialization (Enclosure act) ##### Before Romantic age: Augustan Age - Age of reason and adherence to strict rules - Focus on recreating classical style and harmony - Poetic diction and adherence to rules in language ## William Blake #### The Lamb, The Tyger, London - Poet and engraver committed to highlighting the consequences of the industrial revolution - Two collections of poems: Songs of Innocence and Songs of Experience - Innocence represents the way children approach life, while experience represents the way adults approach life - Symbolism and metaphors used in his poems, simple language ## Wordsworth #### I wandered lonely as a cloud, Composed upon Westminister bridge - Supporter of French revolution and advocate for the return to nature - Nature as a source of inspiration and happiness - Simple language and focus on ordinary situations - Poet as a sensitive observer of hidden beauty ## Coleridge #### Rime of the ancient mariner - Cooperator with Wordsworth in writing Lyrical Ballads - Use of opium influenced his imaginative features in poetry - Rhyme of the Ancient Mariner as a supernatural ballad - Suspension of disbelief and focus on extraordinary events
