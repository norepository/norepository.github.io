[Home](../index.html) # George Orwell - George Orwell was born in India to an English family and later sent to Britain for education. - He studied at Eton, a prestigious and exclusive public school. - Orwell worked as a journalist and conducted social experiments, living among the homeless in London and Paris. - He was politically and socially committed, writing novels like "1984" and "Animal Farm" to represent oppression and tyranny. ## 1984 - "1984" is a dystopian novel set in Oceania, ruled by the Party and its leader, Big Brother. - The protagonist, Winston Smith, works for the Ministry of Truth, where news and information are manipulated. - The Party controls thoughts and actions, using telescreens, posters, and the thought police. - The Party introduces Newspeak, a simplified language to limit thoughts and control the population. - Winston rebels against the Party but is eventually captured, tortured, and brainwashed. ## Animal Farm - "Animal Farm" is an allegorical novel about a farm where animals revolt against their human owner, Mr. Jones. - The animals establish a democratic system based on the principles of animalism. - The pigs, led by Napoleon, gradually take control and become dictators. - Napoleon uses propaganda, violence, and manipulation to maintain power. - The revolution fails, and the animals continue to be exploited while the pigs live luxuriously. ## Connections - Both "1984" and "Animal Farm" explore themes of oppression, manipulation, and the failure of revolutions. - Orwell uses allegory and symbolism to criticize totalitarian regimes and the dangers of unchecked power. - The novels highlight the importance of individual freedom, critical thinking, and resistance against oppressive systems.
